# The Circus of Government Software

If there's one area in which governments excel at wasting money, it is software development.

Digital transformation projects, you know, the ones that let you do paperwork and access government services through websites, consistently cost absurd amounts of money, and it's often because of easily preventable errors like poor planning or hiring exceptionally unqualified people. But we kinds need those projects. By investing in public tech like business registration or property assessment, we save tax money and get more efficient services.

Here is an example: the SAAQ, a state-owned enterprise that issues driver's licenses and regulates vehicles in Québec, somehow found a way inflate the cost of its digital transformation by half a billion dollar and cause an absolute mess when rolling out its new system. At least it gives better service now, you no longer have to wait three hours in a strip mall to sell a car. I'll use this as a case study of how government projects derail, and we'll compare it to other failures to see how the same defects show up elsewhere. And we might learn how public tech that actually works is made!
